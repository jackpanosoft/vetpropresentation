Challenges & Opportunities

Addressing our structural challenges is key to our success. These are not faults, but products of our rapid growth and opportunities for improvement.

One Team, Many Masters

The development team, led by Joey, currently serves four separate masters (New Mexico, VetPro National Maintenance, VetPro National Features, and CalVet). This creates significant challenges in prioritization and work distribution. The team needs a unified reporting structure or a more clearly defined team assignment model.

Clear Communication

The development team has felt that recent work for CalVet and New Mexico was dropped on them without context. High-level decisions are not transparently flowing down, impacting the team's ability to plan and understand the long-term vision. We must ensure development teams are aware of upcoming contracts and their potential impact.

Strapped Resources

There is a large amount of work to be executed over the next two years, which will stretch our current resources.

Team Buy-In

We need to incorporate the development team into the process of defining solutions and execution plans. Their compartmentalized knowledge is critical for making informed decisions and is currently an untapped resource in our planning.

Project Prioritization

While prioritization exists within each project, we lack a clear, interleaved set of priorities across all four development branches. If one team is responsible for all work, we must define a single, unified priority list.

CalVet Threatened

A new competitor, TurboVets, is directly targeting the CalVet market. This threat makes completing the CalVet migration to VetPro National by early 2027 more critical than ever. It is crucial to move customers from our legacy environment, which is more vulnerable to competitors, to the modern VetPro National platform.

Simplified View of VetPro Configurations

A high level view of the different VetPro configurations currently in use and the plan for California's migration.

Standard VetPro

  • Most customers have a single instance of VetPro in which users manage veteran information, complete forms, obtain signatures via the signature portal, and submit forms to the VA's Lighthouse API.

TVC VetPro

  • TVC VetPro is a VetPro configuration used by the Texas Veterans Commission.
  • It is a single instance of VetPro used by multiple agencies across the state of Texas
  • This is our largest single VetPro instance.

South Carolina VetPro

  • South Carolina VetPro is a VetPro configuration used by the South Carolina Department of Veterans Affairs.
  • Each county in South Carolina has it's own VetPro instance.
  • A central application pulls information from each of these instances to populate a customized suite of reports.

New Mexico VetPro

  • New Mexico VetPro is a VetPro configuration used by the New Mexico Department of Veterans Affairs.
  • It is a single instance of VetPro used by multiple counties across the state of New Mexico.
  • A separate application houses a series of custom forms specific to New Mexico.
